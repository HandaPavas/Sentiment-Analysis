Ludhiana : In a major crackdown on the ` Peti ' mafia at the railway station , the investigation wing of excise and taxation department Punjab on Thursday seized 245 cartons of incoming goods , offloaded from three trains , which were being transported into Ludhiana without paying taxes .In the process , the department detected tax evasion worth lakhs , claiming the department to be one of the biggest so far after the implementation of GST .The cartons seized by the department  had electronic goods , mobile accessories and garments .According to officials , the goods have been handed over to railway authorities on supardari as per the GST provisions and owners of the goods  have been given the last chance to present valid tax invoice to the excise and taxation department .Coincidentally the action which was conducted under instructions of joint director of investigation wing H P S Ghotra comes on the day when all the information collection centres -LRB- ICC -RRB- barriers of the state have been permanently done away with .Speaking to TOI about the entire operation , Ghotra said , `` mobile accessories is a major success for the department  .The entire team was working round the clock since yesterday to confiscate the goods  .We have videographed all the 245 cartons and handed all the 245 cartons over to the railways under supardari .An opportunity under Section 129 of Punjab GST and CGST and Section 20 of IGST is being given to the owners of the goods  to present valid bills if tax has been paid on any of all the 245 cartons  .`` Importantly , expecting the presence of taxation officials on the platform , the ` Peti ' mafia at the railway station   did not offload some cartons at Ludhiana station thinking that the ` Peti ' mafia at the railway station would do the same the ` Peti ' mafia at the railway station at Jalandhar station and bring the ` Peti ' mafia at the railway station back to Ludhiana .But seeing a team of officials which was already present on Jalandhar  , the tax evaders did not offload goods there as well and as a result all the 245 cartons  reached Jammu railway station .the investigation wing of excise and taxation department Punjab   has already contacted the authorities at Jammu and requested the authorities to seize the goods  .Other officials who were part of the entire operation  included assistant excise and taxation commissioners -LRB- AETC -RRB- of mobile wing Ludhiana and Jalandhar Magnesh Sethi and B S Bhatti , excise and taxation officers Major Manmohan Singh , Inderjit Nagpal , Davinder Pannu , Pawan Kumar and Sandeep Sharma .` Peti ' mafia still present at station Peti mafia is an organised gang active at Ludhiana railway station and other major stations of Punjab which facilitates the movement of goods to and fro from the railway stations without paying taxes to the government .
