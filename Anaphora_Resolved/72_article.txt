VIJAYAWADA : Union commerce minister , Nirmala Sitaraman on Saturday said that the implementation of GST  is a perfect example of cooperative federalism and added on the request of all the state governments   liquor and petroleum products were not included in the GST list .Sitaraman who was in Vijayawada to take part in an interactive meet said GST rates were fixed in consultation with all the state governments after several rounds of meetings of GST council in which finance ministers of 29 states , seven union territories are members .`` GST council  is a constitution empowered body and GST council met as many as 17 times to discuss GST rates  .GST is a revenue neutral model with an aim that GST should not be a loss either to Centre or states .Rates have been fixed only after all the states have collectively and unanimously agreed .We ministers have been attending interactive meetings on GST at various towns and cities to explain about GST and clarify issues , doubts raised by traders , entrepreneurs and exporters .A similar exercise has been done before July 1 before the implementation of GST , '' said Nirmala Sitaraman .Stating GST  is being implemented to avoid double taxation and cascading effect on the consumers , Nirmala Sitaraman said handloom industries having an annual turnover of Rs 20 lakh need not pay GST  .Nirmala Sitaraman pointed that earlier tax burden was only on consumer and now with the introduction of GST earlier tax burden  is being shared by traders and manufacturers .Nirmala Sitaraman hoped that exports will improve with the introduction of GST which enables free movement of trucks as the toll booths  have been removed across GST  .To a query , Nirmala Sitaraman said some of all the states  will slowly remove the toll booths as some of the states have already agreed to do so .
